How to Create a Professional Invoice

An invoice is more than a payment request. It is a business record that shows what was delivered, what is owed and when payment is expected.

Open Invoice GeneratorLearning Center

What to include in an invoice

A professional invoice should include your business name, client details, invoice number, invoice date, due date, item descriptions, quantities, prices, taxes, discounts, total amount and payment terms.

Best practices

  • Use a clear invoice number for every invoice.
  • Add a due date and payment method.
  • Describe services clearly enough for the client to approve quickly.
  • Keep a copy of every invoice for records.

Common mistakes

Avoid missing client details, unclear descriptions, inconsistent numbering, incorrect tax calculations and vague payment terms.

Tip: After payment, create a receipt so both sides have proof that the invoice was settled.
