Quotation Follow-Up Email Template to Win More Replies

Published June 19, 2026 by DocCraftTools Editorial Team

A quotation follow-up email should remind the customer of the quote, ask if they have questions and make the approval step simple. This guide is written for freelancers, contractors and agencies that want cleaner records, fewer customer questions and documents that are easier to approve.

The focus keyword for this topic is quotation follow-up email template, but the practical goal is simple: help you create a quotation follow-up that is clear, useful and easy to store. You can create a document with the quotation generator when you are ready.

When this document is useful

This type of document is most useful when the business process needs a written record before, during or after payment. Common situations include:

  • following up after a quote is sent
  • checking whether a client needs revisions
  • closing open sales opportunities

For freelancers, contractors and agencies, the value is not only the document itself. The bigger benefit is the trail it creates: what was requested, what was approved, what was delivered, what was paid and what still needs attention. That trail becomes important when a client asks a question weeks later or when bookkeeping records need to be checked at the end of the month.

What to include

A strong quotation follow-up should not make the customer guess what happened or what needs to happen next. Before sending it, check these details:

  • quote reference number
  • project or service name
  • sent date
  • validity date
  • clear call to action

Keep the wording specific but simple. A good business document should be understandable to the customer, the business owner and anyone who later reviews the record for accounting, operations or customer service. Avoid internal shorthand unless the customer already uses the same reference.

Step-by-step workflow

Use this workflow as a simple review process before sending or storing the document.

  1. Wait a reasonable time before following up.
  2. Keep the message short and helpful.
  3. Mention the quotation number.
  4. Offer to clarify scope or pricing.
  5. End with one clear next step.

If the document is connected to another step, such as a quote becoming an invoice or an invoice becoming a receipt, keep the references consistent. Matching document numbers, client names and project descriptions makes it much easier to follow the transaction later.

Example

The exact wording depends on your business, customer and local requirements, but this example shows how to make the record specific:

  • Subject: Following up on quotation QT-2026-019
  • Message: I wanted to check whether you had any questions about the quotation for the website maintenance plan. The quote is valid until July 3, 2026, and I am happy to adjust the scope if needed.

This kind of example works because it gives the reader enough context to understand the transaction without opening a separate email thread. The goal is not to make the document long; the goal is to remove uncertainty from the most important fields.

Common mistakes to avoid

Small documentation mistakes can create payment delays, disputes or messy records. Watch for these issues:

  • pressuring the customer too soon
  • not including the quote reference
  • sending a generic message with no context
  • forgetting the validity date

Most mistakes happen when documents are prepared in a hurry. Building a repeatable format helps prevent missing fields, inconsistent numbering and unclear payment details. A few extra minutes of review before sending can prevent several follow-up messages later.

Record keeping tip

Save the final document as a PDF, use a consistent file name, and keep supporting emails or approvals in the same client folder. If the document connects to another record, such as a quotation, invoice, receipt or delivery note, include the related reference number in your notes.

For better organization, use a naming pattern that includes the date, document number and client name. For example, a simple file name such as 2026-06-19_INV-2026-104_Client-Name.pdf is easier to search than a file called invoice-final.pdf. Consistent names also make it easier to share records with an accountant, bookkeeper or team member.

Frequently asked questions

When should I follow up after sending a quotation?

Many businesses follow up after 2 to 5 business days, depending on the project size.

Should I resend the quotation?

Yes, attach it again or link it so the customer does not need to search.

What should the follow-up ask?

Ask whether they have questions and whether they would like to approve, revise or pause the quote.
