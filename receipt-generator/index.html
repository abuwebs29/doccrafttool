Create a clear proof-of-payment receipt

A receipt confirms that payment has been received. It is commonly used for products, services, rent, deposits, repairs, bookings, reimbursements and private transactions.

This receipt generator helps businesses, freelancers, landlords and individuals create professional proof-of-payment documents. Receipts protect both sides of a transaction because the payer has proof of payment and the receiver has a written record for bookkeeping.

What should a receipt include?

A receipt should include receipt number, payment date, payer, receiver, amount paid, payment method and payment purpose. You can also include order references, invoice numbers, rental periods or notes.

Receipt vs invoice

An invoice requests payment. A receipt confirms payment has already been received. Many businesses use both documents to create a complete transaction record.
